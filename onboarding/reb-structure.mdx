The MUHC Research Ethics Board is not an ad hoc advisory group — it is a formally constituted body with defined membership requirements, governance rules, and institutional accountability. Its composition and operating rules are set by the Minister of Health and Social Services under **Arrêté 2024-017** (_Règles sur la composition et les conditions de fonctionnement des comités d'éthique de la recherche compétents_), the **Cadre de référence ministériel** (MSSS, October 2020), the **TCPS 2 (2022)**, ICH Good Clinical Practice, and — for research involving drugs — the _Food and Drug Regulations_ (art. C.05.001). Locally, the REB is administered through the MUHC **Centre d'éthique appliquée / Centre for Applied Ethics** and governed by **REB-SOP 201.002 — Composition of the REB**.

Understanding the board's structure helps you navigate deliberations, know who to contact for different issues, and appreciate the authority the board holds within the institution.

## Membership Composition

Per SOP 201.002 §5.2, the REB has a **minimum of five (5) members** and must include, at all times, at least one member in each of the following five categories. A single member may fulfil more than one capacity, provided every category is represented.

| Category | Minimum | Requirement (SOP 201.002 §5.2.3) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Research expertise** | ≥ 2 | Members with expertise in the relevant research disciplines, fields, and methodologies covered by the REB. For biomedical clinical trials, at least one must be a **physician, dentist, or pharmacist** in good standing with the MUHC **Council of Physicians, Dentists and Pharmacists (CPDP)**. |
| **Non-scientific member** | ≥ 1 | A member whose primary experience and expertise is in a **non-scientific discipline**. |
| **Ethics expertise** | ≥ 1 | A member **knowledgeable in ethics**. |
| **Legal expertise** | ≥ 1 | A member with **legal expertise, knowledgeable in the laws relevant to the types of research being reviewed** (this is your seat). |
| **Community member** | ≥ 1 | A **community member, or a representative of an organization** interested in the areas of research being reviewed, with **no affiliation** to the institution or the sponsor and who is not the immediate family member of an affiliate. |

<Info>
  **Additional composition rules (SOP §5.2.4–5.2.6):**

  - A single member may satisfy more than one category, but every category must be filled at every convened meeting.
  - Membership must include both men and women, and the majority of members must be **Canadian citizens or permanent residents** under the _Immigration and Refugee Protection Act_.
  - When the REB reviews research involving complementary or alternative care, or pediatric health, at least one member must have relevant expertise in that area. When the REB reviews research recruiting participants from an identifiable Indigenous community, at least one member must be from that community or have equivalent expertise.
</Info>

### REB Chair (SOP §5.4)

The Chair must first be appointed as an REB member and must be an **experienced REB member**. The Chair presides over convened meetings, manages the agenda, signs decision letters, resolves procedural questions, and liaises with the Centre d'éthique appliquée and MUHC leadership. A Vice-Chair or Co-Chair, when appointed, acts in the Chair's absence.

### Alternate Members (SOP §5.3)

The REB may appoint **Alternate Members** who can be called upon to provide additional expertise or to help meet quorum. Alternates:

- Are formally appointed and receive the same orientation and documentation as regular members;
- May **only substitute for a member of comparable qualifications** — for example, a non-scientific alternate may not substitute for a scientific member, and a legal alternate may not substitute for a community member;
- Count toward quorum and vote when they are substituting for an absent regular member (consistent with **TCPS 2 (2022), art. 6.5**).

<Info>
  **Quorum** is the minimum number and mix of members required for the REB to conduct valid business. For the MUHC REB, quorum requires at least five members present and must include **at least one member from each of the five composition categories** (research expertise, non-scientific, ethics, legal, community). A numerically sufficient meeting that is missing the community member, the ethics member, or the legal member is **not quorate**, and no binding decisions can be taken. Your specific quorum formula and any additional requirements (for example, physician presence for biomedical clinical trials) are set out in the REB's operating SOPs.
</Info>

## Voting Rights and Decision-Making

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Voting Members">
    All formally appointed REB members — including you as the member with legal expertise — hold full voting rights. Each member's vote carries equal weight regardless of disciplinary background or professional seniority. Decisions are reached by **consensus** where possible; when consensus is not achievable, the Chair calls a formal vote and the outcome is recorded in the minutes.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Ad Hoc Advisors (SOP §5.5)">
    The Chair or their designee may invite **Ad Hoc Advisors** to provide specialized expertise on a specific protocol (for example, a biostatistician, a pediatric specialist, or a subject-matter expert not represented on the REB). Ad Hoc Advisors:

    - Must sign the MUHC REB **Confidentiality of Information and Conflict of Interest Agreement** before receiving any protocol materials;
    - **Do not participate in deliberations** and **do not vote**;
    - **Do not count toward quorum**;
    - Have their presence, area of expertise, and contribution documented in the minutes.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Observers (SOP §5.6)">
    **Observers** (for example, trainees, visiting scholars, or accreditation reviewers) may attend REB meetings with the Chair's authorization. Administrators or others whose presence might improperly influence deliberations **may not observe**. Observers:

    - Sign the same **Confidentiality of Information and Conflict of Interest Agreement**;
    - Do not participate in deliberations, consensus-building, or voting;
    - May, if the REB determines they are qualified, contribute expert input on a specific point at the Chair's invitation — but they remain non-voting.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Abstentions and Conflicts">
    A member with a conflict of interest on a specific protocol must **declare the conflict**, withdraw from deliberation and voting on that item, and — where the REB's SOPs so require — leave the room while the item is discussed. The declared conflict and the member's absence must be documented in the minutes. Routine abstentions without a declared reason are discouraged because they undermine the board's collective decision-making function.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The REB's Relationship to the Institution

The MUHC REB is simultaneously **part of** the MUHC and **independent from** it. Understanding this tension is essential.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Institutional Accountability" icon="building-columns" href="/onboarding/reb-structure">
    The MUHC establishes the REB, appoints its members through the designated institutional authority, funds its operations, and is ultimately responsible for the board's compliance with Arrêté 2024-017, the Cadre de référence ministériel, the TCPS 2, and applicable law. The REB is administered through the Centre d'éthique appliquée / Centre for Applied Ethics and reports to a designated institutional officer.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Operational Independence" icon="shield" href="/onboarding/reb-structure">
    The TCPS 2 and Arrêté 2024-017 require that the REB operate independently of institutional, political, professional, and financial pressures. The institution may not override an REB decision to refuse, suspend, or terminate research. Researchers, sponsors, and administrators may appeal decisions, but only through the formal appeal process — not by going around the board.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  If you are ever approached by an MUHC administrator, department head, sponsor, or researcher seeking to influence a pending REB decision outside the formal review process, report the approach to the REB Chair immediately. Protecting the board's independence is a collective responsibility, and informal pressure on members undermines the integrity of the entire review system.
</Warning>

## Reporting Lines and Governance

```text
MUHC Board of Directors / Executive Leadership
        │
        ▼
Designated Institutional Authority for Research Ethics
        │
        ▼
Centre d'éthique appliquée / Centre for Applied Ethics
        │
        ▼
Research Ethics Board (Chair)
        │
        ├── REB Members (including the legal-expertise member)
        │   └── Alternate Members (as called upon)
        │
        └── REB Office / Coordinator
                │
                └── Researchers & Study Teams
```

The REB Chair is the primary liaison between the board and institutional leadership. As a member, your formal reporting relationship is to the Chair for all board matters. Day-to-day operational questions go to the REB Coordinator within the Centre d'éthique appliquée.

## The REB Office and Coordinator

The REB Coordinator (also known as the REB Administrator) is the operational hub of the board and sits within the Centre d'éthique appliquée / Centre for Applied Ethics. For newly appointed members, the Coordinator is your most important day-to-day contact.

<Accordion title="What the Coordinator does">
  - Prepares and distributes meeting agendas and protocol packages
  - Assigns protocols to primary reviewers (including routing legal matters to you)
  - Records minutes and issues official decision letters to researchers
  - Manages the REB's electronic submission system
  - Tracks continuing review deadlines, annual reports, and protocol amendments
  - Liaises with researchers to request revisions or clarifications before and after meetings
  - Maintains the official REB records in compliance with institutional retention policies
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What the Coordinator does not do">
  The Coordinator is an administrative professional, not a research ethics expert or legal advisor. They do not make substantive review decisions, provide legal opinions, or resolve disagreements between members. Do not rely on the Coordinator to tell you whether a legal issue is serious — that judgment is yours.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How to work effectively with the Coordinator">
  - Submit your written legal notes and any requests for additional protocol documentation well before the meeting deadline the Coordinator sets
  - Notify the Coordinator promptly if you cannot attend a meeting or if you need to recuse yourself from a protocol
  - Direct routine administrative questions (meeting times, agenda items, document access) to the Coordinator rather than the Chair, who has a full board to manage
  - If you identify a legal issue that requires urgent attention before the next scheduled meeting, contact the Coordinator first — they will escalate to the Chair and determine whether an emergency review is warranted
</Accordion>

## Review Pathways

Not all research goes through the same review process. Understanding the pathways helps you anticipate when your legal input will be most critical.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Full Board Review">
    Required for research involving greater-than-minimal risk, vulnerable populations, novel interventions, or other criteria specified in your institution's SOPs. The full board deliberates and votes at a convened meeting. **This is where your legal review contribution is most visible and most consequential.**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Delegated Review">
    Used for minimal-risk research. A subgroup of the board — typically the Chair or a designated reviewer — reviews and decides without convening the full board. Your institution's SOPs will specify whether legal review is required for delegated files. If a delegated file raises a legal flag, the reviewer should escalate it to full board.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Administrative Review">
    Minor amendments, annual renewals with no protocol changes, and final study completion reports may be handled administratively by the REB office. Legal input is generally not required unless the Coordinator or Chair flags a concern.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Ask the REB Coordinator for the current versions of **REB-SOP 201.002 (Composition of the REB)** and the MUHC REB's other **Standard Operating Procedures**, together with your signed copy of the **Confidentiality of Information and Conflict of Interest Agreement**. These documents are the authoritative source for the REB's quorum formula, appointment terms, voting procedures, review pathways, and member expectations — and they take precedence over any general guidance in this knowledge base.
</Tip>
