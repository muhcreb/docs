Every research study eventually ends — through successful completion, early termination by the sponsor, or suspension by a regulatory authority or the REB. Regardless of how a study closes, the REB is responsible for formally acknowledging the closure, reviewing the close-out report, and confirming that all outstanding ethical and legal obligations have been addressed. As a legal representative on the board, your role at closure is to ensure that the institution is not left with unresolved liability, that participants' ongoing rights are protected, and that the study record is complete and legally defensible.

---

## Types of Study Closure

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Planned Completion" icon="circle-check">
    The study reached its pre-specified endpoint (e.g., target enrolment, follow-up period, or data lock). All participants have completed the protocol or been formally discharged.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Early Termination" icon="stop">
    The study was stopped before reaching its endpoint — by the sponsor, the data safety monitoring board (DSMB), a regulatory authority, or the PI. The reason for early termination is a key legal consideration.
  </Card>

  <Card title="REB or Regulatory Suspension" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    The REB or Health Canada suspended the study, and the PI has determined not to seek reinstatement. Closure in this context carries the most complex legal and regulatory obligations.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What the Close-out Report Must Contain

The PI submits a **Final / Close-out Report** to the REB within the timeframe specified in your institution's SOP (typically 30–90 days after the last participant visit or last data collection point). Review the report to confirm it addresses all of the following:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Study Summary">
    - Total number of participants enrolled, completed, and withdrawn
    - Reason for any early withdrawal at the individual participant level (aggregate, not identifiable)
    - Summary of the overall risk-benefit experience as the study was actually conducted
    - Whether the study was completed as approved or with significant deviations
    - Summary of all protocol deviations and their disposition
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Safety Summary">
    - Aggregate summary of all AEs, SAEs, and UPIRs reported during the study
    - Confirmation that all Health Canada SAE/SUSAR reports have been filed
    - Status of any SAEs that were under investigation at the time of closure
    - Any outstanding safety signals that remain unresolved
    - Whether a final study report to Health Canada (including a final Annual Safety Report, if applicable) has been filed or is pending
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Data and Privacy">
    - Description of where study data (paper and electronic) is currently stored
    - Confirmation that identifiable data has been managed in accordance with the approved privacy plan
    - Description of any plans for future secondary use of the data and whether existing consent covers that use
    - Confirmation that data destruction (where planned) has occurred or is scheduled, with timeline
    - Whether a final Privacy Impact Assessment update has been filed with the institutional Privacy Officer
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Regulatory and Contractual">
    - Confirmation of the final regulatory status (e.g., CTA closure letter to Health Canada filed or in progress)
    - Status of all Clinical Trial Agreements, including any post-study obligations (e.g., publication, data sharing, IP assignment)
    - Whether all investigational product has been returned, destroyed, or transferred to the sponsor
    - Confirmation of final financial reconciliation between the institution and the sponsor (relevant to conflict-of-interest considerations)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## The Legal Representative's Closure Review Checklist

Work through each step before the board signs off on the close-out report.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm Participant Discharge and Post-Study Obligations">
    Verify that all enrolled participants have been formally discharged from the study and that the PI has communicated their discharge status to them in writing. Confirm that:

    - Participants who experienced SAEs during the study are receiving any clinically required follow-up care
    - The consent form's post-study care provisions have been honoured (e.g., continued access to a beneficial treatment)
    - Any obligation to notify participants of final study results (if committed to in the consent form) is scheduled or completed
    - Participants have been informed of any clinically significant incidental findings discovered during the study
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Unresolved Adverse Events">
    Confirm the status of every SAE or UPIR that was open at the time the last participant completed. For each unresolved event:

    - Is the participant receiving appropriate medical follow-up?
    - Has the final causality determination been made and documented?
    - Has the final SAE report been filed with Health Canada?
    - Is there any outstanding liability claim or anticipated claim arising from the event?

    If a liability claim is anticipated or has been made, advise the board that the study file must be preserved indefinitely (or as directed by legal counsel) and must not be subject to routine destruction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess Data Retention Compliance">
    Confirm that the data retention plan is consistent with all applicable legal requirements (see the Note below). Specifically verify:

    - The retention period has been calculated correctly from the correct trigger date (last participant visit, regulatory submission, or data lock — whichever is latest)
    - Identifiable data and anonymised data are subject to their respective retention schedules
    - The data custodian and storage location for the retention period are clearly documented
    - A **secure destruction procedure** is in place for the end of the retention period, consistent with Law 5, Law 25, PIPEDA (where applicable), and institutional policy

    <Note>
      **Record Retention Timelines — Key Legal Requirements**

      Different obligations govern how long research records must be retained:

      - **ICH GCP E6(R3) and Health Canada _Food and Drug Regulations_:** Essential documents must be retained for the periods specified by E6(R3) and applicable Canadian regulations — typically at least **2 years** after formal discontinuation of clinical development or the last regulatory submission, and substantially longer (commonly **15 years** or more) for trials supporting a marketing authorization. Confirm the exact retention period against the current Health Canada–adopted E6(R3) text and the sponsor's essential-documents plan.
      - **Québec — Law 5 and MSSS medical-records retention framework:** Health and social services information must be retained as long as reasonably necessary for the research purpose and in accordance with the establishment's retention schedule and the applicable ministerial framework. Retention schedules for medical records typically require retention for a minimum period after the last professional service (with additional retention where a minor participant is involved, running from the age of majority); confirm the specific institutional schedule.
      - **Quebec Law 25 / Act Respecting the Protection of Personal Information in the Private Sector:** Requires destruction of personal information once the purpose for which it was collected has been fulfilled, unless a longer retention period is mandated by law. A data retention and destruction schedule must be maintained.
      - **TCPS2:** Recommends that REB records related to a study be retained for a minimum of **three years** after the end of the study, but institutional and regulatory requirements frequently mandate longer periods — use the longest applicable requirement.

      Confirm which requirements apply to the specific study and document that analysis in the close-out review record.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Health Canada Regulatory Submissions">
    For regulated studies (clinical trials subject to Health Canada oversight), confirm:

    - A **CTA closure or discontinuation notice** has been submitted to Health Canada, if required under the _Food and Drug Regulations_ or _Medical Devices Regulations_
    - Any remaining Annual Safety Report obligation for the period up to the study's end date has been filed or is scheduled
    - The investigator has notified Health Canada of any SAEs that were still under expedited reporting timelines at the time of closure
    - The sponsor has confirmed in writing whether or not a marketing application will be filed using data from this study (relevant to determining the full GCP retention period)

    <Warning>
      Failure to file a CTA closure notice when required under _Food and Drug Regulations_ C.05.010 leaves the CTA technically active and can create confusion in Health Canada's records. Confirm with the sponsor or sponsor-investigator that the filing has been made and retain a copy of the confirmation in the REB file.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Publication and Data Sharing Obligations">
    Review the CTA and any Data Sharing Agreements to identify post-study obligations the institution has undertaken:

    - Is there a **publication obligation** — a commitment to publish results within a defined period, regardless of outcome?
    - Are there **data sharing commitments** — to a clinical trial registry, a data repository, or other researchers?
    - Are there **embargo periods** that restrict publication pending sponsor review of the manuscript?
    - Are there **co-authorship provisions** that must be followed?

    Confirm that these obligations are recorded and assigned to the appropriate institutional contact. Unmet publication obligations can expose the institution to reputational and contractual liability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Investigational Product Disposition">
    For drug and device trials, confirm that all investigational product (IP) has been:

    - **Returned** to the sponsor, or
    - **Destroyed** in accordance with the destruction protocol approved in the study file, or
    - **Transferred** to another approved facility with sponsor consent

    Obtain written confirmation from the PI or pharmacist that IP disposition is complete. Unaccounted investigational product is a significant regulatory finding at Health Canada inspections.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Issue the Formal Closure Acknowledgement">
    Once all checklist items are satisfied, the REB issues a formal **Study Closure Acknowledgement** letter. This letter:

    - Confirms the date of study closure as recognised by the REB
    - Notes any outstanding obligations the PI has committed to completing (e.g., filing a final publication)
    - Closes the study on the REB's active research register
    - Directs that the study file be transferred to the institution's long-term research records archive

    Retain a copy of the closure letter in the REB's own records for the applicable retention period.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Post-Study Participant Obligations

<Accordion title="Continued Access to Beneficial Treatment">
  Where the consent form committed to continued access to a beneficial investigational treatment after study completion, confirm that the institution and sponsor have a plan to provide that access (e.g., a compassionate use or special access program application to Health Canada). Failure to honour this commitment is both an ethical violation under TCPS2 Article 3.3 and a potential breach of the consent agreement.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Notification of Study Results">
  If the consent form invited participants to request notification of study results, confirm that the PI has a process to honour those requests. Where results are published, provide participants with a plain-language summary if committed to in the consent form. Under TCPS2 Article 3.4, institutions are encouraged to return results of research to participants.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Clinically Significant Incidental Findings">
  Any clinically significant incidental finding identified in a participant during the study must be disclosed to the participant's treating physician (with the participant's consent), even after the study has closed. Confirm that the PI has a documented plan for managing any outstanding incidental findings identified during the study period.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Biological Samples and Biobanks">
  If biological samples were collected during the study, confirm their disposition:

  - Samples consumed in study assays: confirm destruction records
  - Residual samples held for future research: confirm that a biobank agreement and appropriate consent are in place
  - Samples to be destroyed: confirm that the destruction is consistent with the consent form, art. 25 CCQ (non-commercialization), Law 5, and any applicable Québec framework governing human biological material (including the _Act Respecting Human Tissue and Organ Donation_ where relevant)
</Accordion>

## Early Termination: Additional Legal Considerations

Early termination introduces legal complexity beyond that of a planned closure. When reviewing a close-out report for a study that was terminated early, additionally assess:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Reason for Termination" icon="circle-question">
    The reason for early termination is legally significant. Termination for a **safety signal** carries different implications than termination for **business reasons** (sponsor decision). Document the stated reason and confirm it is consistent with any communications to Health Canada.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Participant Notification" icon="bell">
    Participants who were enrolled at the time of termination must be notified and given the opportunity to transition to standard of care or other appropriate treatment. Confirm that the notification process was adequate and documented.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Regulatory Disclosure" icon="file-lines">
    Confirm whether the reason for early termination must be disclosed in clinical trial registry entries (e.g., ClinicalTrials.gov) and whether Health Canada requires a written explanation as part of the CTA closure.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Contractual Consequences" icon="handshake">
    Review the CTA for any provisions governing the consequences of early termination — in particular, IP assignment, data ownership, publication rights, and any indemnification carve-outs related to sponsor-initiated termination.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  For studies terminated due to a safety signal, recommend that the board retain the study file under an enhanced access restriction: only the REB Chair, legal counsel, and the institutional risk management office should be authorised to access the file without a specific written request. This precaution preserves the integrity of the record in the event of future regulatory inquiry or litigation.
</Tip>
